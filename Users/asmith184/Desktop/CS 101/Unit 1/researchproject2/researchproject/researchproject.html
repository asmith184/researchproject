Phishing

Summary

The act of online identity theft that uses email and fake websites that are made to steal personal or sensitive information, such as: credit card numbers, passwords, etc.

What it looks like:

Emails may look like those from sites that you trust.

There is often bad syntax.

May offer coupons or free items.

How to avoid them:

Be aware that companies do not ask for sensitive information, ie: credit card information, passwords, etc.

Legit companies do not send unsolicited attachments.

They often address you by your name, not "dear" or "valued customer".

There are not hyperlinks.

Four interesting facts:

89% of all attacks involve financial and espionage motivations.

49% of non-point-of-sale malware was installed via malicious email.

28% of phishing attacks are targeted.

63% of confirmed data breaches involved weak, default or stolen passwords.

Who started phishing?

The original form of phishing was called “phone phreaking”. Phone phreaking was created by John Draper. Hackers later took hold of this (just on the internet) and out came the word “phishing”.

How this affects online culture:

Online culture is affected by this in numerous ways. As a culture that spends most of its time on the internet, they are constantly inevitably plagued by these emails. It is very dangerous for those who are just getting used to or on the internet for they may not be as aware about the consequences of falling for these emails.
